July 20, 2019 - Linach Dam

Welcome to the first edition of Reservoir Festival for Electric Art.

The festival unifies music and media arts at one unique location in the middle of the Black Forest, Germany. The Linach dam (German: Linachtalsperre) is an architectural masterpiece spanning the valley of the Linach creek at 143 meters.
Over 100 years after the electric revolution, the dam, that elec- trified the region back in the days, now takes on the role as the setting and resource of a festival for electric, electronic and digital arts.

Dedicated to experimenting with new art forms, Reservoirs focus on electric arts is not limited to digital technologies. We seek to provide a stage for a spectrum of audio and video performances, installations directly next to or surrounding the dam, filled with interdisciplinary workshops, all of which encapsulate in some way a reference to the Linach dam and/or its history - past, present or future.

The highlights of the festival include concerts into the night with projections onto the dam, which stretches over the idyllic Linachtal at 143 meters. The festival is dedicated to the 50th anniversary of the first moon landing. Including summer time, it will be exactly 50 years ago at 22:17 this evening that the lunar module of the Apollo 11 mission has landed on the moon.

In the afternoon, the festival begins with a program of audiovisual installations and concerts that is freely accessible to allow a wide audience to play with new media and explore new sounds. For this purpose, students of the University of Trier design several cardboard domes, which are equipped with 360 speaker systems and projections and are recorded with works by invited artists and students of Furtwangen University of Applied Sciences and the Musikhochschule Trossingen. Further installations and performances will be presented in the arches of the Linachtalsperre.

Even before dawn, the evening program begins at around 8 pm with a selection of international artists who bring the Linachtalsperre to a stand with performances of electronic music. After sunset, visual elements are added that are projected across the entire width of the dam. It combines high with low tones, acoustic instruments with electronic music, as well as minimalist abstract forms with bright colorful images - a fireworks of new hearing and seeing.

Behind the event stands a team of students and lecturers from Furtwangen University and members of the non-profit association of the Global Forest. The aim is to create a cultural event at the unique location of the Linachtalsperre, which is becoming a regular meeting place for international artists, and to which a broad and enthusiastic audience from near and far gathers in the Black Forest.

The festival is a non-profit event and focuses on the idea of a collaborative gathering of responsible people. This includes the mutual respect of those involved as well as respect for the environment of the event.
